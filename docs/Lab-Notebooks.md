We have used online lab notebooks since 2007; platforms and workflows have changed over the years. This page covers **expectations** for everyone in the lab. [Comments and suggestions are welcome](https://github.com/RobertsLab/resources/issues).

An online lab notebook is **required** for all lab members. Entries should be organized by date in reverse chronological order and **updated daily**. Document so that you or a stranger could reproduce the work years later. Monthly goals and meeting prep belong in the notebook; see [Project Management](Project-Management.md) and [Environment and Expectations](Environment-and-Expectations.md).

## Platforms

The current recommendation is Quarto in RStudio.

Setup tutorial: [course-fish521 websites lecture](https://sr320.github.io/course-fish521-2023/lectures/week_07/lec_06b_websites.html).

## Document daily

Publish a record of your work the day of activity. If you did little hands-on science, post what you did anyway (papers read, troubleshooting, planning). See [Environment and Expectations](Environment-and-Expectations.md) for how notebooks fit with writing and project goals.

## Backups

Keep a copy of your notebook in another location, for example:

- compose in a text editor and host on GitHub
- use IFTTT to post or save entries elsewhere
- run a script (e.g. `wget`) to archive contents periodically

You may be asked to show that your backup works. GitHub-based notebooks are also mirrored on Gannet: <https://gannet.fish.washington.edu/github_backups/notebooks/>
